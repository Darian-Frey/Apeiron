# Algebra notes

Expanded notes on the algebraic framework summarised in CLAUDE.md §3.

## Topics to cover

- ℤ[φ] as the ring of integers of ℚ(√5); why it is a PID and what that buys
  us for canonical forms and equality testing.
- The icosahedral rotation group *I* (order 60): generator presentation,
  conjugacy classes, and the explicit ℤ[φ]-matrix realisation of each
  element.
- The action of *I* on ℤ[φ]³ and its invariant sublattices.
- Why the natural inflation factor is φ²: eigenvalue structure of
  icosahedral-compatible linear maps.
- Cut-and-project from ℤ⁶ to ℝ³ along the canonical icosahedral slope, and
  why working in ℤ[φ]³ directly is equivalent and simpler.
- Primitivity of a non-negative integer matrix: definition, tests,
  Perron-Frobenius consequences.

All worked examples in this document must use exact ℤ[φ] arithmetic; floats
appear only for numerical sanity-check asides, clearly marked as such.
