### Q11c — Cut-and-project from ℤ⁶ is the better pivot. Most important answer.

**Decoration changes the definition of "tile."** It changes what
Apeiron is searching for, not where. Pillar 4 becomes a stronger
condition (decorations must be locally enforceable). It's a
research-direction change, not just infrastructure.

**Cut-and-project changes only where we look, not what we look
for.** A tile derived from ℤ⁶ cut-and-project is still a geometric
polyhedron that could satisfy Strong Einstein without decorations.
The pivot: search the space of *acceptance domains* (windows) in
the internal space ℝ³_perp.

Three reasons cut-and-project is the right pivot:

1. **Quasicrystal literature has done significant work on which
   projection tilings are aperiodic.** ABCK arises from a specific
   window (dodecahedron). Other windows yield other F-type tilings.
   "Does any F-type projection tiling from ℤ⁶ → ℝ³ admit a single
   prototile?" is well-posed in the existing framework and may
   already have a partial literature answer.
2. **Infrastructure transfer cost is lower than it appears.** ZPhi
   arithmetic, icosahedral symmetry, corona BFS, recognisability
   carry over. ℤ⁶ lattice arithmetic is additive overhead. New
   piece is projection geometry + acceptance-domain machinery.
3. **Substitution-first ⊊ cut-and-project.** Goodman-Strauss §2:
   every substitution tiling under mild conditions is a model set;
   converse not true. Track B's substitution-matrix search is a
   *strict subset* of the cut-and-project search space. Zero-
   Realised may reflect that the right candidate has no
   substitution rule but *does* arise from a non-standard window.

**Recommended (c.2) replacement:** cut-and-project from ℤ⁶ with
variable acceptance domains. Retain marked-monotile as fallback if
cut-and-project also exhausts.

**Pre-build literature read** (analogue of the Goodman-Strauss read

Both short. ~2–3 days reading total.
