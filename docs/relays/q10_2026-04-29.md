## Bug found and fixed mid-sweep

`pf_eigenvector_in_zphi` returns the right eigenvector
(= frequency vector). For volume-ratio matching the left
eigenvector is needed (volume_i ∝ left_eigenvector_i). For
asymmetric M these differ. Fixed by passing M.T to the function;
documented + bug-prevention test added.

**Q10b — Priority order with specific section targets.**

**Goodman-Strauss 1998 first.** Read §3 (local rules and substitution)
and §6 (the extension theorem) in full. Active question: does
Theorem 6.1 (or its corollaries) imply any obstruction to 3D
icosahedral monotile substitution, or does it only establish
sufficiency conditions? The theorem constructs matching rules
from substitution tilings but says nothing directly about whether
a monotile can carry the substitution — the gap between "the tiling
has a substitution rule" and "the prototile is a single shape" is
where an obstruction might live.

Secondary target: §4, "local complexity" results. The zero-Realised
result may be explained by a finite local complexity argument — if
every 3D icosahedral substitution tiling of the relevant type has
unbounded local complexity at the scale of the inflation, no finite
tile set (including a monotile) can generate it.

**Frettlöh second.** Search for his "direct product variation"
(2005–2008) and "tilings with singular continuous diffraction"
(2010–2015) papers. Direct-product variation is most immediately
relevant — the construction takes two aperiodic tilings and produces
a new one; if a 2D aperiodic monotile (hat/spectre) can be combined
with a 1D aperiodic sequence via direct product, the result is a 3D
aperiodic tiling, though not necessarily by a single polyhedron.
Question: can the construction be run in reverse?

Also: Frettlöh and Harriss, "Parallelogram tilings, worms and finite
orientations" (DCG 2013) — for the 15-class completion taxonomy
arguments.

**Socolar third.** Within Socolar 2019, the key section is wherever
he discusses *local* vs *nonlocal* enforcement of aperiodicity — the
distinction between a tile whose shape alone forces aperiodicity
versus one that requires matching-rule decorations. The SCD biprism
uses screw symmetry (nonlocal); the hat uses local shape.
Understanding where our n=3 candidates fall on this spectrum would
clarify whether "no realisation" means "wrong shape family" or
"shape enforcement is insufficient."
