### Q18b — Narrow the title. D₆ in title is one step too wide.

The H₃-fundamental-region ⇒ D₆-projection chain runs from D₆ to
H₃, not reverse. §(ii) establishes NoRealisation across the
9-class H₃ shape slice; the D₆ connection means those shapes
*arise from* D₆ projection, but doesn't mean every D₆-projected
shape family was searched. A reviewer reading "D₆ cut-and-project
framework" in the title will correctly ask "did you search all
D₆ windows, or just the canonical ones?" — the honest answer is
the latter.

**Suggested retitle:** *"Computational Evidence Against 3D
Icosahedral Einstein Tiles Among H₃-Fundamental-Region
Substitution Candidates"*.

Abstract first paragraph locates within D₆ via Theorem 1.3;
§(iii) extends scope to D₆ framing via empire enumeration. The
title→abstract→§(iii) progression mirrors the actual evidential
progression from narrow to broader.

9-of-15-class caveat belongs in §(ii)'s scope statement as a
footnote, not in the title.

### Q18c — Two reads:

**§(i) face-merge no-go.** Rigorous if and only if explicitly
states *positive-integer* eigenvalues required. Two things to
make explicit:

1. The requirement is positive-integer, not just ℤ[φ]. φ³ ∈
   ℤ[φ] and is the PF eigenvalue, but is not an integer. Add a
   one-sentence explicit statement before the eigenvalue list:
   *"For a monotile substitution rule with σ having k children,
   σ(P) must decompose into exactly k copies of P, so k must be
   an eigenvalue of M_ABCK in ℤ_{>0}."*
2. The argument rules out monotile rules *derivable from ABCK
   by face-merge*; it does not constrain monotile rules on
   single-tile alphabets not locally related to ABCK. Add a
   scope-boundary remark immediately after the theorem.

**§(iii.1) argument structure.** Granularity is right; sharpen
the gap statement to the *window-configuration-multiplicity*
level.

The specific combinatorial quantity that varies across windows
is the *multiplicity* of each vertex configuration — how often
each of the 36 types appears — and potentially which subset of
the 36 configurations is *realised* at all in a given window's
tiling. What does NOT vary is the tile shapes themselves
(H₃-rigidity) and the empire structure of each configuration
type that does appear (Hammock Table 1 is window-agnostic for
realised configurations).

**Formal residual-gap statement** to add to §(iii.1):

> "A non-canonical D₆ window could in principle yield a tiling
> in which only a strict subset of the 36 vertex configurations
> appears, and that subset might admit a single-prototile empire
> structure not visible in the full 36-configuration enumeration.
> Resolving this requires knowing which configurations appear in
> the ABCK-specific window, which is the content of
> Kramer-Papadopolos 1994."

Precise, scoped, tells DCG reviewer exactly what remains to be
done. Also the precise statement of what the 1994 ILL resolves
under outcome B.
